History of Bank

  • 1922 - The history of bank starts from the opening of the Central Asian office of "Industrial bank" in Tashkent and is inextricably linked with the development of the country's economy.
  • 1959 - In order to strengthen financial control over the correct spending of funds allocated by the state for capital investments, "Industrial bank" was transformed into a bank for financing capital investments - "Construction bank".
  • 1987 - "Construction bank" was reorganized into "Industrial and Construction Bank".
  • 1991 - After the proclamation of independence, a qualitatively new, two-tier banking system began to form in the country. At the founding conference "Uzbekistan Industrial-Construction bank" was transformed into a joint-stock commercial bank.
  • 1998 - Activities of "Uzbekistan Industrial-Construction Bank" were aimed at the implementation of the program of deepening economic reforms and the State program for ensuring the realization of family interests, approved by the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic, at the implementation of measures to strengthen macroeconomic and financial stabilization.
  • 2006 - It was decided to reorganize "Uzbekistan Industrial-Construction Bank" in the form of joining the Uzbek international bank "Uzbek Private Bank".
  • 2019 - The international rating company Fitch Ratings raised the credit rating of "Uzbekistan Industrial-Construction Bank" from “B+” to “BB-” and assessed its outlook as “Stable”.
